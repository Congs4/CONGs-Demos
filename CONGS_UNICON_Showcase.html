Executive Summary

A dental equipment and consumables distributor operating across Greece and Cyprus through a network of 14 dealers. Leadership needed clarity on commercial performance to inform 2026 planning decisions.

The Challenge

Leadership had access to multiple Excel files across suppliers, dealers, and direct sales channels but lacked a unified view of performance drivers. Key questions remained unanswered: Why were sales declining? Which channels were healthy? Where should they focus for 2026?

The Critical Finding

Volume down 16%, but revenue only down 5%. The volume collapse happened in Budget tier products (61% of volume but only 6% of revenue). The Premium tier - which drives 46% of revenue - actually GREW by 2%. The real concern is the High-Value tier decline (-9%, -141K).

What We Know vs What We Don't Know

What the Data Tells Us

  1. Sell-in volume to dealers dropped 16%
  2. Average transaction value increased 21% (6.12 to 7.41/unit)
  3. Dealer channel revenue stayed flat (+1%)
  4. Non-dealer channel collapsed (-50%)
  5. Budget tier = 61% of volume but only 6% of revenue
  6. Premium tier = 46% of revenue and GREW +2%
  7. High-Value tier declined -9% = -141K
  8. Net revenue change: -145K (-3%)

Data Gaps

  • Sell-through data (what dealers actually sold to end customers)
  • Dealer inventory levels (destocking vs restocking cycles)
  • Whether dealers switched to competitor suppliers
  • Whether overall market contracted
  • Whether same customers buying less or fewer customers buying
  • Root cause of non-dealer channel collapse

The Customer Relationship Risk

Losing budget tier sales may not hurt revenue much today. BUT these everyday purchases keep customers coming back regularly. Risk: If they buy consumables elsewhere, they may eventually buy everything elsewhere.

What the Volume Decline Could Mean

Possible Explanation Implication
Dealers selling to fewer end customers Market share loss at dealer level - concerning
Same customers buying less frequently Demand/consumption decline in market
Dealers destocking inventory Temporary - should reverse in coming months
Dealers sourcing from competitors Losing share with own dealers - very concerning
Overall market contraction Industry-wide issue - not unique to client
Recommendation

Request sell-through reports from key dealers to close the data gap. Even partial data from the top 3-5 dealers would provide valuable insight into whether this is a market share problem, market size problem, or timing problem.

Strategic Questions for 2026

Based on the analysis, we prepared a structured set of questions for the board to address. These questions bridge what the data shows with what the business needs to decide.

Understanding the Volume Decline

Q1 Do we have visibility into dealer inventory levels? If dealers are holding less stock, the decline may be temporary.
Q2 Have any dealers reported losing customers to competitors? Direct feedback would clarify if this is a market share problem.
Q3 Is the budget tier decline across all dealers, or concentrated in specific ones?

Strategic Direction

Q4 Is the shift to premium/higher-priced products an intentional strategy? If yes, budget volume decline may be acceptable.
Q5 Should we invest in getting sell-through data from dealers? This would close the biggest data gap.
Q6 Given Premium tier is 46% of revenue and grew +2%, should we focus there rather than recovering Budget volume?
